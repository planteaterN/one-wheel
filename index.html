Tianjin Eye, China

Is a Ferris Wheel in Tianjin, China. Construction started in 2007 above Yongle Bridge, over the Hai River. The main body was completed on 18 December 2007, and the wheel was opened to the public on 7 April 2008. Tianjin Eye is electrically powered and has 48 passenger capsules, capable of carrying 8 passengers each, and takes 30 minutes to complete a rotation, giving a maximum capacity of 768 passengers per hour.

London Eye, UK

Europe's tallest Ferris wheel, it adjoins the western end of Jubilee Gardens, on the South Bank of the River Thames between Westminster Bridge and Hungerford Bridge, in the London Borough of Lambeth.It was opened to the public on 1 February 2013. It is the most popular paid tourist attraction in the United Kingdom with over 3.75 million visitors annually. As of January 2015, it has been advertised as the Coca-Cola London Eye.

High Roller, Las Vegas

Currently the tallest Ferris Wheel in the world since 2008, standing at 550-foot tall. It was created by the Caesars Entertainment Corporation, for its The Linq promenade area on the Las Vegas Strip. It has a total of 28 cabins, with each cabin being able to accommodating 40 passengers each. High Roller opened to the public on March 31, 2014.
